Help Endangered Animals by Learing About Them

Amur Leopard


Scientific Name: Panthera Pardus Orientalis


Status: Critically Endangered


Habitat: Native to the snowy mountain of
South Eastern Russia and Northern China.


Fun Fact: Amur leopards have a summer
and winter coat.

Blue Whale


Scientific Name:Balaenoptera musculus

Status: Endangered


Habitat: Along the West Coast of the United States,
eastern North Pacific blue whales are believed to
spend winters off of Mexico and Central America.


Fun Fact: The blue whale is the largest animal
on the planet, weighing as much as 200 tons
(approximately 33 elephants).

Giant Panda


Scientific Name: Ailuropoda melanoleuca


Status: Vulnerable


Habitat: in temperate forests high in the
mountains of Southwest China


Fun Fact: baby pandas are born pink

Orangutan


Scientific name: Pongo abelii, Pongo pygmaeus


Status: Critically Endangered


Habitat: are found only in the rain forests
of the Southeast Asian islands of Borneo
and Sumatra.


Fun Fact: we share 96.4% of our DNA with
these great apes

Polar Bear


Scientific Name: Ailuropoda melanoleuca


Status: Vulnerable


Habitat: Arctic Ocean, sea ice,
and adjacent coastal areas.


Fun Fact: Polar Bears have 3 eyelids The third helps
protect the bear's eyes from the elements

Tiger


Scientific Name:Panthera tigris


Status: Endangered


Habitat: rain forests, grasslands, savannas and
even mangrove swamps. Across Asia


Fun Fact: tigers are the largest cats in the world
